Kitchen Remodeling:
Editing and Relighting Intricate Indoor Spaces from a Single Panorama

We propose a novel inverse rendering method for remodeling the existing kitchens from a single panorama. Our method includes four key components: (1) HDR photography for indoor-outdoor panoramas, (2) estimating complex 3D room layouts, (3) editing the furniture layout for the kitchen area, and (4) global rendering method that relights the new kitchen space within the outdoor panorama. Additionally, we contribute the Pano-Pano HDR dataset with 140 scenes and present a low-cost photometric calibration for each HDR photograph.

An indoor panorama showcases a scene with kitchen and living spaces. The existing kitchen area is remodeled with a new dark gray background wall and a new set of cabinets and appliances. The gray-blue cabinets reflect the detailed scene on their surfaces, while the refrigerator with metal surfaces shows fewer reflective effects. For the living space, the existing furniture is removed, and the room is refurnished with a new set of furniture.

Overview

We present a complete pipeline from data collection to scene editing and relighting. First, we use a single camera, Ricoh Theta Z1, to capture paired indoor and outdoor panoramas. Second, the indoor panorama is used to estimate the 3D room layout, while the outdoor panorama serves as a 360-degree environment map. Third, the kitchen area is segmented from the indoor panorama. Appliances and furniture objects are positioned along the kitchen wall. Finally, the new kitchen space is rendered under global illumination with a complete scene model.

Editing Kitchen Layout

In real-world scenarios, kitchens exhibit a wide range of dimensions and configurations. To customize the remodeling process, we first developed a detailed geometric model of the cabinets and appliance sets. Each object is assembled from various components, such as handles, cabinets, and countertops, allowing for material customization in the rendering process.

Pano-Pano HDR Dataset

In this study, we contribute a new Pano-Pano HDR dataset that includes 140 paired indoor and outdoor panoramas. We introduce a low-cost photometric calibration for each HDR image and evaluate its accuracy compared to the standard approach.
